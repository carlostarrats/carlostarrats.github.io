The Challenge

Users without an account often bounced immediately after submitting a Request for Quote (RFQ) from the product page.

Background

RFQ's are product/price requests submitted from a product page when stock is unavailable or the price doesn't meet a users expectations. They are pretty common within the electronics sourcing industry.

The Goal

Enable RFQ submissions from guest users and logged-in users alike — so we reduce abandonment at the login barrier and preserve high-value quotes.

The Approach

We designed a revised RFQ submission flow that explicitly recognises two key user states: signed-in and guest.

By mapping the journey and isolating the drop-off point at login, we uncovered opportunities to streamline the experience for guest users while preserving the return experience for account holders.

The Solution

A unified flow where:

  • Signed-in users proceed as expected.
  • The interface clearly segments “Submit as guest” vs “Log in / Create account” without disrupting conversion.

    This choice balances immediate user needs with long-term account growth.

Business Impact

With ~120 RFQs submitted weekly and ~80 coming from logged-out users, ~40 of those were dropping off at login — translating to ~$1,500 × 40 = $60K per week (~$3M/year) in potential lost value.

This redesign was handed to the dev team for implementation, positioning the business to significantly improve conversion and revenue capture.
