The Challenge

Eloquii’s legacy e-commerce platform struggled to capture the brand’s core message — fashion is transformative.

User data showed that shoppers often scrolled past the homepage slider but rarely engaged further. Visual hierarchy and content flexibility were limited, and internal teams relied heavily on design and engineering to make even small updates.

The result: a slow, inconsistent experience that underrepresented the brand and frustrated teams trying to keep up with fast-moving campaigns.

The Goal

Redesign the shopping experience to:

  • Reflect Eloquii's brand identity.
  • Empower non-technical teams to update content dynamically.
  • Improve engagement and conversion across devices.
  • Create a unified, flexible design system that supports speed and scale.

Research & Insights

Customers were scrolling down the page but were not clicking through toward the end of the header slider. The drop-off past the first image was significant with the following image / marketing not having visibility.

The Approach

I led the design of a modular, scalable system that gave Eloquii creative freedom while maintaining visual coherence. The focus was on reusable components, mobile parity, and empowering internal teams to experiment safely within consistent boundaries.

  • Defined a modular homepage architecture that could adapt to campaign needs.
  • Introduced messaging and promo slots that could change layout and imagery without new code.
  • Designed responsive defaults so mobile experiences automatically simplified complex layouts.
  • Unified the visual language across web and iOS to reflect the same brand personality everywhere.

Key Design Decisions

  • Modular homepage slots — each slot could host a single horizontal hero image or up to three stacked vertical cards.
  • Sticky promo bar — persistent across the site to highlight urgency messaging like free shipping or limited-time offers.
  • Dynamic layout control — teams could select 1–3 boxes per promo area; mobile defaulted to a single rotating message.
  • Unified system styling — typography, grid, and spacing harmonized across every page template.
  • CMS flexibility — enabled marketing to swap creative and messaging without involving engineering.

Refinements

Implementation of a flexible homepage that accounts for layout changes to maximize photo assets, provide newness for repeat customers, and mark differentiation for event days.

Sales and shipping are a big point of focus for conversion for the brand. As such we established a flexible slot controlled by the business team. The slot is able to hold 3, 2, or 1 "box" with the 1 being a rotating message of up to 3 messages if desired. Mobile and tablet automatically default to the 1 box rotating messages regardless of the desktop experience. In addition, on scroll the slot is sticky across desktop, tablet, and mobile for full visibility site-wide to drive urgency.

Outcome & Learnings

The redesign empowered Eloquii’s internal teams to iterate faster and more independently.

Campaigns launched quicker, visual identity stayed consistent, and the shopping experience became simpler and more cohesive.

While I can't share exact performance metrics, qualitative feedback showed:

  • Reduced dependency on developers for daily updates.
  • Improved homepage engagement and campaign turnaround time.
  • Stronger alignment between product, marketing, and design teams.

The project re-established Eloquii’s site as a living reflection of its brand rather than a static storefront.
