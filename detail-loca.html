The Challenge

Buying art is often intimidating and inaccessible. Artists struggle to show and sell their work, while everyday buyers rarely discover local art outside of gallery settings.

The Goal

Make discovering and purchasing local art as simple and natural as noticing a piece on the wall.

The Approach

We designed a direct, QR-based system linking physical artworks to their digital listings. Visitors could scan, learn more, and purchase instantly—no gallery, no barrier.

To get there, I led design and product definition from 0–1:

  • Defined core user journeys for each audience (artist, space, buyer).
  • Designed the complete purchase flow and onboarding experience.
  • Collaborated with the founding team to align product vision with launch constraints.

The Solution

A mobile-first marketplace where:

  • Artists connect easily with spaces and showcase their work.
  • Spaces enrich their environments while supporting local creators.
  • Buyers scan, learn, and purchase directly from the wall.

The experience centered on clarity and immediacy, making the act of buying art feel as seamless as appreciating it.

Key Design Decisions

  • QR-first interaction — replaced traditional product discovery with contextual discovery in real spaces.
  • Streamlined onboarding — simplified artist registration from multiple forms to a single guided flow.
  • Unified visual system — designed consistent language across artist, venue, and buyer interfaces.

Outcome

LoCA has grown steadily, expanding from Seattle to Chicago as the team continues to explore product–market fit. The MVP validated strong interest among local artists and spaces, and the team is refining its model to better serve each audience. The platform’s simple, QR-driven experience remains its foundation — connecting art, place, and people with minimal friction.
