The Challenge

Kikoff lets users build credit by making low-cost digital purchases that count as recurring payments. But the existing ebook store functioned mainly as a placeholder to enable that mechanism. After signup, 40% of users dropped off at the store — they didn’t see the content as useful or engaging, so they didn’t return.

The brief: transform a compliance-driven utility into a marketplace users actually want to use, without breaking the credit-building model.

The Goal

Redesign the marketplace so that users gain real value from what they buy — educational, entertaining, or interactive — while keeping the credit-building mechanism intact.

Research & Insights

We spoke with users and internal stakeholders to understand the gap between function and engagement.

Key takeaways:

  • Time is a problem: users don't have time to sit down and read.
  • Users find the ebooks boring: the content is not engaging enough to hold their attention.
  • Users want answers: users want relevant answers when they need them. Solutions vs. knowledge
  • Users want progress: reading a book does not produce results. They want more gratification.

These insights became the foundation for reimagining the marketplace.

The Approach

I explored new product directions that could make the Kikoff marketplace genuinely engaging:

  • Conversational learning — a chatbot-style interface that personalizes recommendations and makes financial education interactive.
  • Quiz-based experiences — short, gamified modules that deliver insights through action.
  • Modular content design — enabling new categories (courses, micro-learning, digital assets) to replace the static ebook model.

Each concept balanced user value with credit-building compliance requirements.

Key Design Decisions

  • Introduced multi-format content modules to support different engagement types.
  • Built a lightweight conversational prototype to test tone and flow.

Outcome & Learnings

Although the project didn’t reach full public release due to platform constraints, the work established a clear direction for the product’s evolution:

  • Validated user interest in more interactive and meaningful purchases.
  • Produced a prototype library of three distinct marketplace models tested with real users.
  • Informed the company’s future roadmap for higher-value engagement loops.
